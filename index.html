What is STEM

STEM stands for science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. All 4 factors work together in the world and they all make a great impact and as students develop a deep understanding of scientific principles, technological advancements, engineering design processes, and mathematical concepts they will be able to apply these skills in real-world scenarios. The learning of STEM begins a passion for exploration and discovery. Students are also encouraged to ask questions, conduct experiments, and seek solutions to complex problems. STEM is a hands-on approach which starts the curiosity and keeps a love for learning and solving new problems, an important skill that is needed in the real world.

STEM's Importance

STEM is becoming more important in our daily lives, almost every career includes a factor in STEM. With the new improvements in the world with the advance technology, we must advance with it. STEM helps develop creativity and 21st-century skills including media and technology literacy, productivity, social skills, communication, flexibility and initiative.
